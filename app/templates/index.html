What is it?

CAUSE & EFFECT

Causes

When talking about Earth’s climate change it is important we understand what greenhouse gases are. Greenhouse gases are gases in our atmosphere that absorb infrared radiation and as a result, they hold and trap heat. We will go over 3 types of greenhouse gases, how they get released in our atmosphere and how they impact our environment. These should give you an idea of how greenhouse gases, in general, get produced and easily create changes to our environment.

  • Nitrogen

    As the most abundant gas in our atmosphere, it plays an important role for farmers. It is essential for plant growth asit can be used to make fertiliser and boost crop yeilds. However, farmers in the U.S. waste around 40% of the nitrogen they use in their crops. Other culprits include vehicle exhaust, power plant exhaust, and large-animal feeding operations

  • Carbon Dioxide

    CO2 is released through natural processes such as respiration and volcano eruptions and through human activities such as deforestation, land-use changes, and burning fossil fuels.

  • Methane

    This hydrocarbon gas is produced both through natural sources and human activities, including the decomposition of wastes in landfills, agriculture- especially rice cultivation. as well as manure management associated with domestic livestock.

Effects

On average, the Earth will become warmer. These temperatures will lead to more evaporation and precipitation overall. A stronger greenhouse effect will warm the oceans and melt glaciers, increasing sea level. Ocean water will also expand (since water expands as it warms), further contributing to a rise in sea level. Higher temperatures and shifting climate patterns may change the areas where crops grow best and affect the makeup of natural plant communities.

Facts

  • Global climate change has already had observable effects on the environment. Glaciers have shrunk, ice on rivers and lakes is breaking up earlier, plant and animal ranges have shifted and trees are flowering sooner.
  • Effects that scientists had predicted in the past would result from global climate change are now occurring: loss of sea ice, accelerated sea level rise and longer, more intense heat waves.
  • The major greenhouse gases are water vapour, which causes about 36–70% of the greenhouse effect; carbon dioxide (CO2), which causes 9–26%; methane (CH4), which causes 4–9%; and ozone (O3), which causes 3–7%
  • Satellite observations reveal that the amount of spring snow cover in the Northern Hemisphere has decreased over the past five decades and that the snow is melting earlier.
  • Global sea level rose about 8 inches in the last century. The rate in the last two decades, however, is nearly double that of the last century and is accelerating slightly every year.
